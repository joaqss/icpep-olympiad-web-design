## Brief Explanation of Design Decisions
We started with a simple question: Why does the web feel so static when life feels so fluid? Most websites are a series of abrupt jumps—click a button, wait for a blink, see a new page. We wanted to break that "wall" between the user and the content. So, we looked at the Microsoft PowerPoint Morph effect. Morphing isn't just moving; it's an evolution where one idea physically transforms into the next. That became our light-bulb eureka moment, creating a website that doesn't just show information but tells a continuous story in a scroll.
 
To bring this vision to life while maintaining a clean, minimalist aesthetic, we blended the ethereal depth of Mesh and Aurora gradients with subtle parallax effects. By utilizing organic, continuous line work, we've created a visual narrative that guides the user through the complex beauty of Computer Engineering—inviting them to see the field not just as code and circuits, but as an evolving landscape of innovation. We wanted to give students a window into the heart of our discipline.
